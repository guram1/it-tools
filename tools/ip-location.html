About IP Location Detection

Your IP address is a unique identifier assigned to your device by your Internet Service Provider (ISP). It reveals information about your approximate location, network provider, and can be used to customize content and services.

What Information Does Your IP Reveal?

  • Geographic Location ‚Äî City, region, and country (approximate, not exact address)
  • Internet Service Provider ‚Äî The company providing your internet connection
  • ASN (Autonomous System Number) ‚Äî Identifies the network your connection routes through
  • Timezone ‚Äî Based on your geographic location
  • Connection Type ‚Äî Whether you're on residential, business, or mobile network

Common Use Cases

  • Network Troubleshooting ‚Äî Verify your public IP for firewall rules and port forwarding
  • VPN Verification ‚Äî Confirm your VPN is working and showing the correct exit location
  • Remote Access ‚Äî Find your IP to set up remote desktop or SSH access
  • Geolocation Testing ‚Äî Test location-based services and content delivery
  • Security Audits ‚Äî Verify what information is exposed about your network

IPv4 vs IPv6

This tool shows your public IPv4 or IPv6 address depending on your network configuration. IPv6 addresses are longer (e.g., 2001:0db8:85a3:0000:0000:8a2e:0370:7334) and are becoming more common as IPv4 addresses become scarce.

Privacy Considerations

While your IP address reveals approximate location, it does not reveal your exact street address, name, or other personal information. However, your ISP can connect your IP to your account. Using a VPN can mask your real IP address.
